Notes

I took a lot from the videos in the "Master Mobile UX" course. The main takeaway being "whatever you're doing, do mobile first." As I watched the video I noticed that many websites have adapted to this way of thinking which has lead to many user frustrations. It is important to remember that mobile is not a fad and it is not going away. I also like learning about ways to improved mobile UX. 1. Be there - cater to the user's needs. 2. Be useful - give them what they came for. 3. Be quick - smooth and seamless transitions. This can be accomplished by: 1. reducing image usage throughout the site/app. 2. optimizing and minifying css & js files 3. GZIP files to eliminate unnecessary data. Overall, this video really made me excited for what this semester holds!
